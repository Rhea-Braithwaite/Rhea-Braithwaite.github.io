WalkWithYou

Overview: Mobile application that provides users with tools to make their traveling journey safer.
Developed as part of The Summer Environmental Education Development (SEEDs) program, an annual intensive 10-week mobile app development hackathon. It provides an opportunity for female college students to gain coding experience and work with a non-profit.

I was paired with another participant, Jasmine, and we worked together on each phase of the app development.

Early Steps

We wanted to explore the problem domain of Women’s Safety, operating from our own personal experiences as young women navigating daily life. After conducting initial research it was statistically clear, this wasn’t isolated to us and very much universal.

Problem Statement

The final question we landed on as our problem statement then became: How might we help females or people, feel safer and more protected when traveling?

Target Group and User Interviews

We used this baseline to nail down our primary user base (women) and conducted interviews to outline their transportation journey and identify paint points our app could improve.

Ideation and Prototyping

This led to the app’s key features and their specific offerings:

Feature Offering
Emergency call buttons Pre-saved emergency contacts and messages, to immediately convey that you are worried for your safety.
Phone call simulator Using pre-recorded conversations so you can simulate talking to someone over the phone
Self-defense resources Learn self-defense skills on the go, so you can use them in the event of an emergency

We then worked on low-fi prototype sketches of each feature

and then transformed them into high fidelity versions in Figma.

Final Product

I then coded the final app in Android Studio using the Kotlin programming language.
